In the Game of Stones, a player can remove 1, 2, or 3 stones per turn. Using combinatorial game theory, the losing positions are multiples of 4. If n % 4 == 0, the first player cannot force a win and loses if the second player plays optimally. Otherwise, the first player can always win by reducing the pile to a multiple of 4 on the next turn.
